# TalentPoint: The Confluence Model

## Product Concept — v3 (April 2026)

**A Confluence Point Consulting Product**

---

## The Problem

School leaders manage talent across five or six disconnected streams — different vendors, different logins, different spreadsheets, different mental models. No tool connects them. The result: leaders operate on gut instinct, spreadsheets, and scramble.

The education HR tech space is crowded with tools that do one thing:

- **HRIS platforms** (Frontline, PowerSchool) track who's in what position
- **ATS platforms** (AppliTrack, Nimble, SchoolSpring) process applicants
- **Survey platforms** (Attuned, TNTP Insight, UpBeat, Possip) measure how people feel
- **Evaluation systems** capture formal observations for compliance

Nobody connects them. Nobody helps leaders **act** on the full picture. And two critical streams — multi-year staffing plans and daily leadership activity — aren't captured by any tool at all.

---

## The Insight

**TalentPoint is the confluence of six data streams that school leaders currently manage in their heads.**

The name says it: Confluence Point Consulting built the tool that brings the streams together.

## The Six Streams

| # | Stream | What It Is | Exists Today? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **HRIS — Staffing Reality** | Active positions, who's in each seat, certifications, grade levels, buildings | Frontline HRMS, PowerSchool — siloed |
| 2 | **ATS — Hiring Pipeline** | Open postings, applicant pools, offer stages, acceptances | AppliTrack, Nimble — siloed |
| 3 | **Survey & Engagement Data** | Motivation profiles, engagement scores, pulse results, feedback | Attuned, TNTP, UpBeat, Possip — siloed |
| 4 | **Staffing Chart — This Year + Next Year** | Current positions AND projected positions side by side, enrollment-driven, budget-driven | **Nobody. Spreadsheets.** |
| 5 | **Retention Signals** | Intent to return, stay conversation outcomes, flight risk indicators, exit data | **Nobody. Gut instinct.** |
| 6 | **Leadership Activity (Lead Measures)** | Daily touchpoints, informal walk-throughs, coaching notes, recognition, stay conversations, observation follow-ups | **Nobody. Lost in hallways.** |

### The Key Distinction

- **Streams 1-3** exist but are siloed across vendors. TalentPoint integrates them.
- **Stream 4** doesn't exist as a tool. TalentPoint builds it.
- **Stream 5** doesn't exist as a tool. TalentPoint builds it.
- **Stream 6** is the lead measure that drives outcomes in all other streams. It's the daily work of leadership. It's captured nowhere. **TalentPoint lives here.**

## What Makes This Different

### It's not a better mousetrap.

Every tool in the K-12 HR space does one of two things:

1. **Administrative compliance** — track positions, process applications, store evaluations (Frontline, PowerSchool)
2. **Periodic measurement** — survey teachers, generate reports, deliver insights (TNTP, UpBeat, Attuned)

TalentPoint does neither. TalentPoint is the **operational layer** where insight meets action.

### The category is new: Talent Operations for Schools.

Not HRIS. Not ATS. Not survey platform. Not evaluation tool. The convergence point where all of those streams meet — and where school leaders actually do the work.

### Surveys are lag measures. TalentPoint captures lead measures.

Surveys tell you how people felt last month. TalentPoint captures the daily activity that *changes* how people feel next month: the walk-through, the coaching conversation, the positive note, the stay conversation. Over time, this becomes the most predictive data in the system.

### Retention-first, not recruiting-first.

The entire K-12 HR tech space is focused on the front door (recruiting). TalentPoint focuses on the back door (retention). Recruiting is the fallback when retention fails — and when it does, TalentPoint handles that seamlessly too.

## What It Feels Like

### For a principal on a Tuesday morning:

- Open the app. See your building. Not a dashboard — your **building**.
- A nudge: "You haven't checked in with Marcus Davis in 3 weeks. He's in a shortage cert area."
- Tap. Log a 30-second note after a hallway conversation. Done.
- A flag: "Intent-to-return forms are due in 6 weeks. 4 teachers haven't been asked."
- Swipe through your staff. See the stream — engagement data, your touchpoints, their trajectory.

### For a principal on the worst day:

- "Sarah just resigned." Swipe her position. Resignation logged.
- The system knows: she taught 7th grade math, she held a SPED endorsement, there were 4 applicants for similar roles last year in this region.
- Approval chain fires. Posting drafts. Pipeline activates.
- Next year's staffing chart updates automatically.
- You're back in the hallway in 2 minutes.

### For a CMO on a Monday:

- Network view. Six schools. Each one shows the confluence — staffing health, retention signals, pipeline status, leadership activity levels.
- One school is quiet — low touchpoints, no stay conversations logged, two teachers in shortage areas haven't been engaged.
- That's the school that's about to have a spring attrition problem. You can see it before it happens.

## The Annual Cycle

The tool understands the rhythm of a school year. The UI, the nudges, the priorities shift based on where you are:

| Season | Focus | TalentPoint Does |
|---|---|---|
| **Aug–Oct** | Onboard & stabilize | New hire check-ins, early warning on bad fits, 30/60/90 touchpoints |
| **Nov–Jan** | Invest & retain | Stay conversations, recognition prompts, mid-year pulse, engagement nudges |
| **Feb–Mar** | Detect & act | Intent to return, flight risk surfacing, proactive retention moves |
| **Mar–May** | Plan & recruit | Next year staffing chart, pipeline activation, resignation workflows |
| **Jun–Jul** | Fill & prepare | Hiring pipeline, offer management, onboarding prep |

The season isn't a feature. It's the **backbone** of the product.

## Integration Strategy

**Build natively (streams 4, 5, 6):**
- Multi-year staffing chart (this year ↔ next year)
- Retention signal tracking (stay conversations, intent to return, flight risk)
- Leadership activity capture (daily touchpoints, walk-throughs, coaching notes, recognition)

**Integrate (streams 1, 2, 3):**
- HRIS data via adapters (Frontline, PowerSchool, Workday, CSV import)
- ATS data via adapters (AppliTrack, Nimble, or build lightweight native posting)
- Survey data via partnerships (Attuned preferred partner, TNTP/UpBeat/Possip as additional sources)

**Partner — Attuned (preferred, parallel paths):**
- Day-one integration: pull Attuned motivation profiles and nudges into TalentPoint's people view
- Parallel: build lightweight school-native touchpoint and pulse capabilities so TalentPoint is never dependent on a single partner
- Over time: TalentPoint's own operational data (Stream 6) becomes a proprietary insight layer

## The Moat

1. **Operational data nobody else has.** Stream 6 — daily leadership activity — creates a proprietary dataset. Over time, TalentPoint can show: "Schools that log 3+ touchpoints per teacher per month before February have 40% less spring attrition." No survey company can generate that insight because they don't have the operational data.

2. **Network effects.** As more schools use TalentPoint, the pattern library grows. Labor market signals sharpen. Benchmark data deepens. Each new school makes the platform smarter for every school.

3. **Switching cost through integration.** Once TalentPoint is the confluence point connecting HRIS + ATS + surveys + staffing plans, ripping it out means going back to disconnected silos.

4. **The annual cycle lock-in.** TalentPoint becomes the operating rhythm of the school year. Leaders plan around it. It's not a tool they check — it's how they work.

2. **Formal observations/evaluations** — integrate from existing eval systems (6a) or build lightweight capture natively? State requirements vary widely. Could be complexity trap.

3. **Pricing model** — per school? per network? per seat? Freemium for individual principals?

4. **First customer** — Greenline Talent Group as channel partner? Direct to a charter network? Scott's relationships in the sector?

5. **Name availability** — "TalentPoint" has conflicts in general HR space. USPTO trademark (Marriott) is dead/cancelled. Need to secure domain (.app likely available) and potentially file fresh trademark in education SaaS class.

6. **Attuned partnership** — what does a real integration look like? API access? Co-marketing? Revenue share?
